How How the Atlanta Falcons serve as a role model in creating a Leed Stadium.

Leed stands for the "Global standard for measuring the Sustainability of a building (...)"
It is important for every industry to develop a throughout Sustainability program to save the Environment

The following items need to be considered when creating a sustainable Stadium:
  • Recycle most materials (cups, plates, fan articles, etc.)
  • Make Public Transportation easily accessible
  • Less Meat for food before games
  • Digitalize the ticketing
In addition, there needs to be an emphasis on the materials used to built a stadium:
  1. Efficient energy usage
  2. Sustainable energy sources (solar pannels, reusable water, etc. )
  3. Planting trees outside the stadium

Not only the stadium of the Falcons is a great example.

Places all around Europe are examples of sustainable projects.

The biggest waste mashines in modern sports:

According to the EPA, an average college football game produces anywhere from 50 to 100 tons of waste for packages

Green Game plan:

Establishing recycling programs for each of the materials generated is the best way to maintain their quality. Aluminum cans, and plastic & glass bottles are among the recyclable materials that are generated the most by fans. Commingled recycling captures the value of the containers and keeps them separate from fibrous materials. We recommend that all the bins are placed strategically throughout the stadium in high traffic areas, such as near restrooms, hallways, and concession stands so fans can conveniently access them.
